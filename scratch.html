My Favorite Books

Here are some of the books that have meant the most to me, organized by the way they have impacted my life:

  1. Little One (A Novel)
    By Olivia Muenter

    A young couple's life is disrupted when they take in a mysterious and unsettling girl connected to the husband's past. As strange and increasingly disturbing events unfold, the wife begins to question the child's true nature and her husband's secrets, leading to a tense psychological unraveling.

    • Genre: Psychological Thriller
    • Best for: fans of dark, suspenseful fiction
    • My rating: 5 out of 5 stars
  2. The Rose Code
    By Kate Quinn

    Set during World War II, three female codebreakers work at Bletchley Park, deciphering enemy messages. Bound by secrecy and friendship, their relationships are tested by betrayal, love, and the pressures of war. Years later, a shocking revelation forces them to confront the past and uncover the truth behind a long-hidden betrayal.

    • Genre: Historical Fiction
    • Best for: history lovers and mystery fans
    • My rating: 5 out of 5 stars
  3. The Hunger Games
    By Suzanne Collins

    In a dystopian future, Katniss Everdeen volunteers to take her sister's place in a deadly televised competition where only one tribute can survive. As she navigates alliances, survival, and manipulation by the Capitol, Katniss becomes an unexpected symbol of resistance against a cruel and controlling government.

    • Genre: Dystopian Fiction
    • Best for: anyone who loves action and strong heroines
    • My rating: 5 out of 5 stars

Benefits of Reading Daily

Science backs what book lovers have always known. Here are some proven benefits of reading every day:

Builds Vocabulary

Regular reading exposes you to new words and sentence structures.

Reduces Stress

Just 6 minutes of reading can reduce stress levels by up to 68%.

Builds Empathy

Fiction helps you understand the perspectives of others.

Improves Focus

Reading trains your brain to concentrate for longer periods of time.
